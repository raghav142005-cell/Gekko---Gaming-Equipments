## What's in this file

A complete, end-to-end high-fidelity mobile app design for Gekko —
a gaming equipment shopping app built for the Indian market.

Screens included:
- Welcome / splash animation
- Home page with Shop by Theme sections
- Product category pages (controllers, keyboards, mice, headphones)
- Product description page with the Personality System
- Cart and checkout flow
- Order confirmation and tracking
- About Us and policies pages (Privacy, T&C)

---

## The Project

Gekko is a gaming peripherals app designed for one specific gap:
most gaming platforms are built for people who already know what
they want. Gekko is built for everyone else too.

The central design problem was trust — not aesthetics. Users in
research avoided buying gaming gear online because product
information was too technical, pricing was deceptive, and there
was no social proof. Gekko addresses this with plain-language
product descriptions, upfront pricing, in-app order tracking,
and a reviews system.

The standout feature is the **Product Personality System** — each
product is introduced with a character and voice instead of a
spec sheet, so any user regardless of technical knowledge can
immediately understand what a product is for.

## How it was built

**Timeline:** ~2 months · June – August 2025
**Tool:** Figma (wireframes → hi-fi prototype)
**Process:** Google UX Design Framework

1. **Empathize** — Moderated usability study, 6 participants,
   mix of gamers and non-gamers, Jaipur
2. **Define** — 3 personas, user journey maps, problem statements
3. **Ideate** — Explored quiz flows, comparison tools, AI assistant
   concepts, and the product personality direction
4. **Prototype** — Lo-fi wireframes first, then full hi-fi in Figma
5. **Test** — Second round of usability testing, 4 participants,
   hi-fi prototype — insights fed back into final design

---

## Research summary

**Round 1 (lo-fi) — 6 participants**
- 4/6 found existing gaming sites too technical and cluttered
- 3/6 preferred buying offline due to lack of trust
- 6/6 wanted a gaming AI assistant feature
- 4/6 wanted specs in plain language

**Round 2 (hi-fi) — 4 participants**
- 2/4 surprised by hidden charges at checkout → fixed upfront
- 2/4 flagged missing reviews section → added as Priority 0
- 3/4 confused by some labels → microcopy revision queued
- 3/4 preferred authentic in-hand product photos
